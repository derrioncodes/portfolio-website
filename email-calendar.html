Email Calendar

Overview

In this project we are building an email campaign calendar for the month of August. It is for a brand called Wilde, they are healthy snack brand that sells protein chips made from chicken breast, egg whites and bone broth to help with overall health and wellness while eating a delicious snack.

Their target audience are people who care about eating healthy and want a snack they can enjoy without worrying about macronutrients.

Creating the Email Calendar

Creating an effective monthly email campaign calendar is like creating a roadmap for success. It ensures our campaigns are timely, relevant, and aligned with our audience's needs and the brand's business goals.

To start building a campaign calendar, we need to focus on these four key questions:

  • What holidays or events are happening this month?
  • Does the brand want to run any sales or promotions?
  • Are there specific products or collections the brand wants to highlight?
  • Are there any new releases that need to be covered?

Answering these questions will give us a strong foundation for our monthly calendar. Next, we will think about other value-driven micro-topics that educate and nurture the audience while positioning the brand's products as solutions to their needs. These additional topics will help fill out the rest of the calendar and create a well-rounded campaign plan.

For an effective campaign calendar, we aim to send 3‑4 emails per week. This ensures the brand remains top of mind with their subscribers without overwhelming them. The focus should be on delivering value-driven content so that our emails build trust and loyalty, rather than relying heavily on discounts that could eat into the brand's profitability. If subscribers feel like we're always trying to take something from them instead of offering value, they may start ignoring your emails altogether.

By creating a thoughtful, balanced campaign calendar, we can keep the audience engaged, build strong relationships, and drive meaningful results for the brand.
